BrainStation Projects

In 2024, I completed a diploma in Web Development at BrainStation. Although I had been teaching myself web development for a long time, and even had work experience, I still felt like the gaps in my knowledge were holding me back.

When I had the good fortune of winning a scholarship for the BrainStation course, I knew this was my opportunity to master the skills that had been eluding me. These are some of the projects that I built for the course.

Byte-Sized Medieval Paleography

View the source code

My final project for the BrainStation diploma was a game that teaches you how to read medieval handwriting.

This was an idea I'd wanted to make for a very long time, and a resource I wish existed when I was teaching university students to read old handwriting.

Tech stack:

  • Front end: React, HTML, SASS
  • Back end: Express, Node
  • Database: MySQL

If you want to try it out, here's the live demo.

WordPress Sites

Between 2021 and 2023, I customized WordPress sites for businesses while working at a digital marketing agency.

I enjoyed learning more about the clients' needs and creating websites that matched their visions.

This experience eventually led me to enroll in a web development diploma course so I could master the skills to build even more complex websites.

Auxilium Mortgage Corporation

Auxilium Mortgage needed a modern, responsive website to showcase their award-winning team and positive reviews.

The client chose a theme, which I customized by installing plugins to display their reviews and to create staff profiles.

These features highlight their achievements and make them more approachable to potential customers.

I designed page layouts, styled add-ons, configured plugins, and migrated the content from their old site to their new one.

After the final review, I trained the client to use the site and all its new features.

On this project, I used:

  • WordPress
  • PHP
  • JavaScript
  • HTML
  • CSS
